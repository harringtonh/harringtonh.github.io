Heather’s Signature Crêpes!

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • ¼ cup water
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional)

Directions:

Mix thoroughly. Put pan on medium high heat. Melt a dab of butter in pan. Pour batter in and sqirl around until coating all of bottom. Wait until all top goes from being white to being yellow as it cooks. Then flip. Count for 30 seconds and remove with spatula. Makes 5 crêpes. Serve with jam, berries, nuttela, powdered sugar, and honey. For savory, pesto, fresh mozzarella, tomato, carmalized onion, roasted bell pepers, and balsamic glaze.

Spacey Dutch Baby

Ingredients:

  • 3 large eggs, at room temperature
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup whole milk, at room temperature
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Pinch of nutmeg
  • toppings!

Directions:

Heat oven to 425 degrees. Combine eggs, flour, milk, sugar and nutmeg. Place butter in a heavy 10-inch skillet and place in the oven. As soon as the butter has melted (watch it so it does not burn) add the batter to the pan, return pan to the oven and bake for 20 minutes, until the pancake is puffed and golden. Lower oven temperature to 300 degrees and bake 5 minutes longer. Eat with many fruit, jam, nutella, powdered sugar etc.

Sarah’s Sweet Challah

Ingredients:

  • 2 ¼ cups of bread flour
  • 1 ¼ tsp salt
  • ¼ cup white sugar
  • ¼ cup canola oil
  • 1 ½ tsp honey
  • 1 egg
  • ½ cup water
  • 1 teaspoon yeast

Makes one loaf of the size pictured above; that’s about 14-16 inches long.

Directions:

With bread machine: Combine solids (flour, salt, and sugar) then liquids (oil, honey, egg, and water) in the bread machine pan. Add yeast last, or in the yeast dispenser. Put on the dough setting. (This will finish the first rise). Remove the dough from the machine and punch it down. Separate the dough into equal 4 parts, and roll out into a log (I find squeezing into a log often to be easier). Braid the loaf. Place the loaf on a cookie sheet lined with parchment paper. Mist with water (I usually just sprinkle with my fingers), cover loosely (plastic wrap or a towel, though sometimes a towel sticks), place in a warm place (I heat my oven to like 95 and then put it in) and let rise until doubled in size-- usually 1 to 1 ½ hours. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. Beat one egg--you can add a tsp of water if you want. Brush the loaf with this egg wash. Bake for 20-25 minutes-- until the loaf sounds hollow when you tap on the bottom.

Without bread machine: Dissolve yeast in water with a pinch of sugar-- let stand until frothy on top (5-10 minutes). Combine dry ingredients (flour, salt, sugar) in a bowl. Make a well in the dry ingredients and add wet ingredients (egg, oil, honey); beat them together into a slurry. Only small amounts of flour should be added at this step. Add the yeast into the wet ingredients. Mix the whole thing with a wooden spoon until a shaggy dough forms. Mix with a dough hook/mixer for 6-8 minutes on low speed, or turn the dough out onto a floured work surface and knead dough for 10 minutes. If the dough seems very sticky, add teaspoons of flour until it seems tacky, but not crazy stretchy like bubblegum. It’s done when it is smooth and can hold shape as a ball. Let the challah rise for 1 to 1 ½ hours, until doubled in bulk. From here, it’s the same as after having removed dough from the bread machine -- see from step 2 above.

Susan's Fudgy Brownies (w/ cakey varient*)

Ingredients:

  • 4 oz. unsweetend chocolate (Heather note: double!)
  • 1 ½ sticks butter
  • 3 eggs
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 tsp. vanilla
  • 1 cup flour

Directions:

Grease a 9 x 13 inch pan. Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Melt chocolate + butter together in microwave or on stovetop. Beat eggs in electric mixer until foamy. (Heather: if beating by hand just do your best) Add sugar and beat until thick and creamy. Add melted chocolate + butter and vanilla and mix until incorporated. Beat in flour. Pour into pan - bake for 30-35 minutes.

*For cakey brownie, add 1/2 cup milk with the butter + chocolate; increase flour to 1 1/2 cups.

Moist Zucchini Bread

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/2 cups grated zucchini - lightly packed -do not drain liquid
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup packed light brown sugar
  • 3/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all purpose flour
  • 3/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 3/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Directions:

1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. Spray a 9x5 inch loaf pan with cooking spray. 2. In a large bowl, add the grated zucchini, sugar, brown sugar, applesauce, oil, eggs, and vanilla. Whisk until well combined. 3. Add the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon. Stir just until no dry flour remains, trying not to over mix. 4. Pour the batter into the loaf pan. Bake for about an hour. check it. A toothpick inserted into the center of the bread should come out with moist crumbs on it. 5. Cool in the pan for 10 minutes and then eat warm!

Alejandro's Tarta de Santiago (Spanish Almond Cake)

Ingredients:

  • 4 eggs
  • 1 cup (200 grams) caster (superfine) sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla
  • 1 lemon, zested
  • 2 cups (192 grams) almond flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon Diamond Crystal kosher salt

Directions:

1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. 2. Cut out a circle of parchment paper to fit the bottom of a 10-inch tart pan with a removal bottom. Grease the tart pan generously with butter. Place the parchment circle in the bottom of the pan and grease that generously with butter as well. 3. In a large bowl, vigorously whisk together eggs, caster sugar, and vanilla until slightly pale and aerated. 4. With a spoon or rubber spatula, stir in salt, lemon zest, cinnamon, almond flour, and hazelnut flour. Set aside. 5. Pour batter into your prepared tart pan and place that on a baking sheet. Place into your preheated oven and bake for 30 minutes. 6. Remove cake from the oven and let it cool on a cooling rack for at least half an hour. Press up on the bottom of the tart pan, releasing the cake from the tart pan. Transfer cake back onto the cooling rack and if using the stencil, place in the middle of the cake. Dust generously with powdered sugar and remove stencil carefully to reveal the iconic cross in the center of your Tarta de Santiago.

Sonia's Nutmeg Logs

Ingredients:

    COOKIES

  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 1 cup butter, softened
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla
  • 2 teaspoons rum extract
  • 1 egg
  • 3 cups all purpose unbleached flour
  • 1 teaspoon nutmeg

    • FROSTING

  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 3 tablespoons butter, softened
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla
  • 3/4 teaspoons rum extract
  • 2 to 3 tbls half-and-half or whole milk
  • nutmeg

Directions:

1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. 2. In large bowl, combine sugar, 1 cup butter, vanilla, rum extract, and eggs. Beat until light and fluffy. 3. Add flour and nutmeg. Mix well. 4. Cover dough with plastic wrap and refrigerate for about 45 minutes. 5. Divide dough and roll into long rods about 1/4 in diameter. Cut into 1 inch segments. Place on uncreased cookie sheet. 6. Bake for 12 to 15 minutes, or until edges are light golden brown. Immediatly remove from cookie sheets and place on wire rack to cool for about 20 minutes. 7. In a small bowl, combine all frosting ingreedients except nutmeg, adding just enough milk for good spreading consistency. Spread on top of cookies. Sprinkle with nutmeg. Makes about 15 dozen. Originally from Rebecca Shue DeSensi

Grace's BBB (Browned Butter Brookie)

Ingredients:

    BROWNIE

  • 8 tablespoons/113 grams unsalted butter, plus more for greasing the pan
  • 1 cup/201 grams granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1+1/2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup/64 grams all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup/71 grams cocoa powder, Dutch-process or natural
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt (Diamond Crystal)
  • 1 tablespoon espresso powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon

    • COOKIE

  • 6 tablespoons/85 grams unsalted butter (browned)
  • 1 cup/220 grams packed brown sugar, light or dark
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1+1/4cups/160 grams all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 3/4 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1+1/4 cups/215 grams chocolate chips or chopped bar chocolate

Directions:

1. Heat oven to 350 degrees. Grease and line a 13-by-9-inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving a 2-inch overhang on two sides. 2. Prepare the brownie layer: In a medium saucepan brown the butter over medium heat for 10-12 minutes. While it cooks, the butter will foam, pop, and crackle. This is normal! Stand by and stir it occasionally. When the bottom of the pan has brown bits of butter solid and it starts to smell nutty, scrape all of it into a separate bowl. 3. Add sugar and whisk vigorously until well combined. 4. Once the mixture has cooled a bit, whisk in the eggs one at a time. Whisk in the vanilla. 5. Add the flour, cocoa powder, espresso powder, cinnamon, baking powder and salt, and stir to combine. Transfer to a medium bowl. 6. Prepare the cookie batter: Wash and dry the saucepan. brown the butter over medium heat for 10-12 minutes. While it cooks, the butter will foam, pop, and crackle. This is normal! Stand by and stir it occasionally. When the bottom of the pan has brown bits of butter solid and it starts to smell nutty, scrape all of it into a separate bowl. 7. Add sugar and whisk vigorously until well combined. 8. Once the mixture has cooled a bit, whisk in the eggs one at a time. Whisk in the vanilla. Add the flour, baking powder and salt, and stir. Stir in ¾ cup of the chocolate. 9. Using two cookie scoops, drop each of the batters into the prepared pan. The pattern can be random, and one dough can be on top of the other; just make sure that once all the batter is added to the pan, you have an even thickness spread out across the pan. Sprinkle the remaining ½ cup chocolate on top. 10. Bake until the top looks dry and set, and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out with moist crumbs attached, 20 to 22 minutes. 11. Transfer to a rack to cool completely, about 45 minutes. Lift the bar out using the paper and transfer to a cutting board. Cut into bars to serve.
